Targeted Treatment Strategies

Based on our detailed inspection, we will develop a tailored treatment plan designed to specifically address your infestation. We utilize a range of highly effective treatment methods.

Liquid Termiticide Treatments

We often employ liquid termiticides to create a protective barrier around your property. These products are applied to the soil surrounding the foundation, providing an invisible but potent defense that termites cannot detect and avoid. As termites attempt to cross this treated zone, they are exposed to the termiticide, which affects them and can be transferred to other termites in the colony, leading to colony elimination. Modern liquid termiticides are highly effective and designed for long-term control.

Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer an alternative approach to termite control. These systems involve placing bait stations in strategic locations around your property. The bait contains a slow-acting substance that termites consume and carry back to their colony, effectively eliminating the colony over time. Baiting systems can be particularly effective for monitoring termite activity and controlling colonies without the need for extensive liquid treatments.

Direct Wood Treatment

In situations where termites are actively feeding within wood structures, we may utilize direct wood treatments. These treatments involve applying termiticides directly to infested wood, targeting the termites where they live and feed. This can be a crucial step in eliminating active colonies within the structure itself.

Fumigation (Less Common for Subterranean Termites)

While less common for the subterranean termites prevalent in our area, fumigation is a treatment method used for certain types of termites, particularly drywood termites. This involves tenting the structure and introducing a fumigant gas that penetrates the wood to eliminate the pests. If our inspection indicates the need for this type of treatment, we will discuss it as an option.

Identifying the Threat: Common Termite Issues in Midland, TX

Being aware of the signs of a termite infestation is the first step to protecting your property. Termites are often called 'silent destroyers' because they can cause significant damage before being noticed. In Midland, TX, subterranean termites are the most common concern. Here are some key indicators to watch for:

Mud Tubes on Foundation or Walls

Subterranean termites build narrow, pencil-sized mud tubes, also known as ""shelter tubes,"" as pathways between their underground colonies and their food source (your home's wood). These tubes protect them from predators and dry air. If you discover these tubes on your foundation, crawl space walls, or even inside your home, it's a strong indication of subterranean termite activity. We will carefully inspect and remove these tubes, then treat the areas to prevent further tunneling.

Discarded Swarmer Wings

Termite colonies produce 'swarmers' – winged reproductive termites that emerge to establish new colonies. This often happens during warmer months after rain. If you find small, translucent wings, often near windowsills, doors, or light fixtures, it suggests termites have swarmed from a nearby colony, potentially even within your walls. We can identify the type of termite and determine if the swarming originated from your property.

Damaged Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving a thin veneer of paint or surface wood untouched. This can make damage difficult to spot initially. Tapping on wood surfaces with a screwdriver or similar tool might reveal a hollow sound where termites have been active. Look for warped wood, buckling floors, or ceilings that appear to be sagging. Our inspections are designed to identify subtle signs of wood damage, even in less accessible areas.

Clicking Sounds from Walls

While not always audible, some people report hearing faint clicking sounds coming from within walls infestated with termites. This sound is believed to be the soldiers hitting their heads against the wood to signal danger to the colony. If you suspect termite activity and hear unusual noises, a professional inspection is warranted.

Frass (Termite Droppings)

Drywood termites, though less common than subterranean termites in Midland, expel their droppings, called frass, from small 'kick-out' holes in the infested wood. Frass resembles tiny pellets or sawdust and may accumulate in small piles below the infested area. Identifying frass helps us determine the type of termite and the location of the infestation.

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

Bubbling or peeling paint that doesn't seem to be caused by water damage could be another sign of termites. Termites tunneling beneath the surface can create moisture and disrupt the paint's adhesion, leading to a blistered or peeling appearance. We can investigate the source of such issues to rule out or confirm termite involvement.
